History of Tekken

*Tekken* is a series of fighting games developed by Bandai Namco Entertainment, first released in 1994.

It has become one of the most popular and long-standing fighting game franchises in the world, known for its deep mechanics and diverse roster of characters.

The series has evolved through multiple installments, each introducing new features, characters, and storylines.

First Release: Tekken (1994) Latest Release: Tekken 7 (2017)
  • Tekken 3 (1997)
  • Tekken 4 (2001)
  • Tekken 5 (2004)
  • Tekken Tag Tournament 2 (2011)
  • Tekken: The Motion Picture (1998)
Game Mechanics

The *Tekken* series features a robust fighting system that includes 3D movement, a combo system, and blocking/countering techniques.

Players can enjoy various modes of play, including Arcade, Versus, and Story modes, enhancing the gameplay experience.

Key mechanics include:

Combo System Blocking and Counters
  • 3D Movement
  • Character Abilities
  • Environmental Interactions
  • Rage System
  • Online Multiplayer
Character Types

Characters in *Tekken* are categorized into different types, such as Brawlers, Grapplers, Technical characters, and Speedsters.

Each type has unique playstyles that cater to different fighting strategies.

Some examples of character types include:

Brawlers Speedsters
  • Brawler: Paul Phoenix
  • Grappler: King
  • Technical: Hwoarang
  • Speedster: Asuka Kazama
  • Hybrid: Jin Kazama

Best Practices

To excel in *Tekken*, players should practice combo timing, learn matchups, watch tournaments, and adapt their playstyles based on opponents.

Regular training and analysis of gameplay can significantly improve skills.

Training Mode Watching Replays
